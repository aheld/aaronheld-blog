# Social Hooks: Same Architecture, One Hour Each

## Candidate openings for LinkedIn

### Hook 1: The surprising tie (recommended)
Video for explaining architecture has gotten cheap enough that I now reach for it a lot. To test how far that's come, I documented my Market Finder harvester two ways: a written C4 doc, and a five-minute animated video I generated from that doc with Fable. Each took about an hour. That tie surprised me enough to distrust it, and distrusting it is where the real lesson was.

### Hook 2: The confession
I finished a complete architecture doc, diagrams and a few thousand words, and then noticed I'd written a confession into the last section: the part of this system that matters most is the part the diagram can't show. So I handed the doc to Fable and got the version that moves. Here's what comparing the two taught me about documentation media.

### Hook 3: The contrarian angle
"Just make a video" is bad advice for most architecture docs, and I say that having just generated one I'm happy with in an hour. The animation research is skeptical of motion, video can't be diffed or grepped, and it starts going stale the day it renders. Here's the narrow case where it still earns its hour, and why the written doc stays canonical.

### Hook 4: The three-layer cost
Same architecture, documented as a doc and as a video, one hour each. But "one hour each" is a trap: it only held because the code was already documented and my video pipeline already existed. The honest cost of architecture-as-video has three layers, and only one of them is a tie.
