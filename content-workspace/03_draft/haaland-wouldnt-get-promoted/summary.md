# Summary: If Erling Haaland Were a Software Engineer, He'd Never Get Promoted

## SEO Description (1–2 sentences)

Norway's World Cup hero fails every activity metric we track. What Haaland's walking says about war rooms, dashboards, and who gets promoted.

## Impact Statement (3–4 sentences)

The deadliest striker on Earth spends most of every match walking, and by every activity metric engineering teams track he would look disengaged. This piece uses Haaland's World Cup run to name a promotion-system failure most engineering leaders participate in without noticing: rewarding visible firefighting over the quiet work that prevents fires. It gives managers language for defending their quiet high-performers in calibration, and one honest prescription: the business scoreboard exists, so do the attribution work of connecting quiet effort to it instead of counting whatever's easiest. Grounded in a real promotion fight, a QA lead punished for preventing bugs, Bill Atkinson's -2000 lines, and MIT's "Nobody Ever Gets Credit for Fixing Problems That Never Happened."
