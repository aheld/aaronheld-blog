# Recurring Themes

**ICM Layer**: L1 (domain). Living document — evolves as the blog grows. Eventually feeds Hugo categories and tags.

## How this file grows into taxonomy

This is a **living list** of themes Aaron returns to. The progression:

1. **A thread appears once** → noted here as an emerging theme.
2. **A thread appears 3+ times** → graduates to a Hugo `category` (canonical, broad, few in number).
3. **A thread appears 10+ times** → load-bearing `tag` territory.

This keeps Hugo categories and tags honest rather than ad-hoc per post. Don't add a category to `config.yml` until a theme has earned it from the writing.

## Active themes

### The social contract between companies and employees

The implicit deal — what employer and employee owe each other — and how it's been quietly rewritten while we weren't looking. Layoffs as "performance management," the disappearance of pension-era loyalty, the rise of "your job is your responsibility" framing.

- **Connects to**: middle-manager survival, "nothing unprecedented" (compare to past labor cycles)
- **Hugo category candidate**: yes, when 3+ posts directly engage

### Middle-manager survival and "do no harm"

How to be a manager who shields without being a doormat, who amplifies without being a megaphone, who builds people up while the surrounding system tries to grind them down. The player-coach model. The shield and amplifier.

- **Connects to**: social contract, AI-as-amplification (managers don't get replaced, they get freed)
- **Hugo category candidate**: yes, central thread

### "Nothing we are seeing is unprecedented"

The historical-parallel move — current pattern X mirrors earlier pattern Y. Examples already in the blog:
- AI changes programming ↔ public cloud + DevOps changes
- Modern documentation crisis ↔ pre-AI accessibility/inclusion lapses
- Modern caching debates ↔ the cache wars of the 2000s
- Layoff cycles ↔ prior labor cycles

**Note**: this is also a *signature move* (see `signature-moves.md`) — it's both a recurring theme and a recurring technique.

### Process as servant, not master

Process is supposed to serve outcomes. When it becomes the point, the system fails. Recurring observation across agile, planning, governance, and management contexts.

- **Connects to**: middle-manager survival, AI-as-amplification

### AI as amplification, not replacement

What new tooling — including AI — actually changes vs. what the hype claims. The thread that shows up in posts about middle managers, programmers, and writers.

- **Connects to**: nothing-unprecedented, social contract

### Psychological safety as foundation

Performance, innovation, and retention all rest on people being able to speak honestly. Recurring theme even when not the explicit topic.

### The aging professional / staying engaged

Career longevity, staying technical while leading, the player-coach model, lifelong learning as habit not project.

- **Connects to**: middle-manager survival, specialization-is-for-insects

### Knowledge sharing vs. gatekeeping

Documentation, accessibility, mentoring — all forms of refusing to gatekeep. The opposite is "ask me," which is fragile and exclusionary.

### Specialization vs. generalism

The Heinlein quote, the warrior-vs-soldier distinction, the cost of over-specialization in scaled engineering organizations. Aaron has explicitly returned to this — there's a "specialization-is-for-insects-part-ii" post.

When a post is in stage 2 (outline) and seems to engage a thread not listed here, add a new section above with:
- One-line description
- "Connects to" pointers to related themes
- "Hugo category candidate" assessment

Don't bloat the list — themes should be ones Aaron *returns to*, not just topics that came up once.
