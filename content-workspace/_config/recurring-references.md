## Science Fiction

### Robert A. Heinlein
- **Anchor quote**: "A human being should be able to change a diaper, plan an invasion, butcher a hog, conn a ship, design a building, write a sonnet, balance accounts, build a wall, set a bone, comfort the dying, take orders, give orders, cooperate, act alone, solve equations, analyze a new problem, pitch manure, program a computer, cook a tasty meal, fight efficiently, die gallantly. Specialization is for insects."
- **Used in**: "Specialization is for Insects" (and a part II).
- **Next time think about**: posts about over-specialization, generalist value, T-shaped engineers, fragmented teams.
- He also never believed in shared and continual SciFI worlds, believing that the setting for each story should be unique for that story

### Fredric Brown — "The Weapon" (1951)
- **The setup**: A scientist who built a doomsday weapon, sleeping easy, until a stranger arrives and hands his developmentally-disabled child a loaded revolver.
- **Used in**: "The Weapon by Fredric Brown" (with Rod Serling framing).
- **Next time think about**: posts about technology consequences, hubris of creators, AI release decisions, "we built it, now what" arguments.

### Rod Serling / The Twilight Zone (framing device, not specific story)
- **Used in**: "The Weapon" — italicized narrative opener in Serling's voice, pivoting to "You are now entering... the Twilight Zone" then dropping into the actual post.
- **Next time think about**: posts where a fable framing earns its keep — irony, consequences, moral inversions.

### Issac Asimov Robots series
- **Next time think about** how the 3 laws of robotics ensured they were benevolant towards thier creators.  Contract to our AI that is only constrained by heavily lobbied against regulation.  Bolos by Keith Laumer is related.

## How to use this file

When entering stage 1 (research) for a new piece:

1. Skim this file for any name, work, or anchor that could speak to the topic.
2. Pull the relevant ones into `01_research/[slug]/sources.md`.
3. If the topic genuinely doesn't connect to anything here, that's worth noting — does the post belong on this blog, or is it a one-off?
4. After publish, update this file: did the piece introduce a new author/work that's likely to recur? Add it.

The file is honest: many slots are empty. Fill them as the writing demands.
